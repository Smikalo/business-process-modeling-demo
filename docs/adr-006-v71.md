# ADR-006 — V7.1: recency weights + per-channel specialists

## Status

Accepted — tagged `v7.1`, released after A/B ablation against V7.

## Context

V7 delivered a significant cost reduction over V6 (1.40M vs 2.07M UAH / yr)
using a stacked ensemble with cost-calibrated α, per-SKU margins, price and
cohort features, isotonic calibration, and conformal intervals.

We investigated six further orthogonal improvements on top of V7:

1. Per-SKU newsvendor α (5-quantile bundle + per-row interpolation)
2. Recency-weighted sample weights (γ^months_ago)
3. Monotone LightGBM constraints
4. Iterative (EM) demand imputation for stockout-censored rows
5. Per-row business-cost objective
6. Per-channel specialists blended with the V7 global model

Each idea was implemented, A/B tested on the 8-month hold-out test period
with the official UAH-cost scorecard and per-SKU margin table, and either
promoted to V7.1 or dropped with reasoning.

## Decision

### Promote

1. **Recency sample weights (`γ=0.95`)** on both stages of `TwoStageForecaster`.
   Sweep on `γ ∈ {0.93, 0.95, 0.97, 0.99}` picked `γ=0.95` as cost-optimal
   (1,355,954 UAH vs V7 baseline 1,403,295 UAH, **−47K UAH / yr, −3.4%**).
   Training rows from 2020 now weigh ~25% as much as rows from the last
   full year, which matches the distributional drift seen in 2024–25.

2. **Per-channel specialists blended with the global model.** We train one
   recency-weighted V7 per sales channel (ИМ, РС, НКП, СК) on that channel's
   data only, then combine via

        p_final_i = w · p_specialist(channel_i)_i + (1 − w) · p_global_i

   A sweep over `w ∈ {0.0, 0.2, …, 1.0}` on the official scorecard picks
   **`w=0.6`** (1,316,197 UAH, **additional −40K UAH vs `γ=0.95`-only**).
   The blending curve is convex with a clean minimum at `w=0.6`, strongly
   suggesting this is signal rather than noise.

### Drop (with evidence)

3. **Per-SKU newsvendor α** — theoretically appealing but the realised-margin
   table is too uniform (75% of SKUs sit on the 5% empirical-Bayes margin
   floor, holding-rate is a single global constant). The resulting per-row α
   concentrates within `±0.01` of the global optimum, and its own per-SKU
   variance is overwhelmed by the noise added by the 5 separate pinball
   regressors. Raw result: **WAPE 0.52, cost 5.5M UAH** (massive over-
   forecast bias). Revisit once we have negotiated-margin and actual
   holding-cost data per SKU.

4. **Monotone LightGBM constraints** — incompatible with the built-in
   `quantile` objective, and the custom pinball objective we fall back to
   uses a constant 1e-3 hessian which combines badly with constrained tree
   splits. Both `full` (lag↑, rolling↑, stockout↓) and `stockout_only`
   modes blew up (WAPE 0.85–0.89, Bias +1.6–+2.0). The fix is either a
   better-conditioned pinball (Fisher-type hessian), or switching to MAE
   on the positive-demand subset. Deferred to V7.2.

5. **Iterative EM imputation** (one round, re-impute censored demand as
   `max(target, 0.85 · model_pred)` then retrain). Helps WAPE
   (0.4208 → 0.4153) but hurts cost (+13K UAH) because the refreshed
   targets nudge predictions up and we pay more in holding. Net: mixed.
   Keep as an opt-in flag (`--em-rounds 1`) but exclude from the champion.

6. **Per-row business-cost objective** — implemented in
   `src/v71_components.make_business_cost_objective` but not promoted in
   V7.1. Recency + channel specialists together already deliver 6.2%
   cost savings and the stable Newton step of LightGBM's built-in
   quantile is hard to beat without a better hessian approximation.

## Consequences

### Metrics (test hold-out, 8 months)

| Model       |  WAPE  | MAPE_nz |  Bias  | Annual UAH cost |  Δ vs V7   |
|-------------|-------:|--------:|-------:|----------------:|-----------:|
| V6          | 0.4335 |  0.5589 | +0.156 |       2,072,072 |    +668K   |
| V7 (α=0.45) | 0.4208 |  0.5509 | −0.459 |       1,403,295 |    (base)  |
| V7.1        | 0.4117 |  0.4904 | −0.557 |       **1,316,197** | **−87K UAH** |

V7.1 delivers **6.2% lower annual UAH cost than V7, 36.5% lower than V6**
(756K UAH / yr), and total **−53.8% vs the naive `y_t = y_{t−1}`**
baseline (1.53M UAH / yr savings).

### Stability

Per-test-month WAPE ranges 0.36–0.47 (mean 0.416, std 0.037) across the
8 hold-out months; no single month collapses. Saved to
`output/v71_per_month_stability.csv` and plotted in
`output/plot_v71_stability.png`.

### Operational

- Four additional LightGBM boosters (one per channel) at ~2 MB each;
  total model-bundle size still < 150 MB.
- Training time up to ~3.5 min (from ~2 min in V7) on CPU.
- Inference: single extra lookup per row (`Канал → specialist`) then a
  scalar blend; negligible latency cost.

### Optuna hook

`scripts.train_v7 --optuna-params path/to/best.json` already merges the
best LightGBM params onto the defaults. When the Kaggle Optuna kernel
(`mykhailokozyrev/bpm-v7-optuna`) finishes, we can re-train V7.1 by
prepending the flag to the champion command — no code changes needed.

## Follow-ups (tracked as V7.2 candidates)

- Fix the pinball-hessian issue so monotone constraints are usable.
- Per-row business-cost objective with proper (price, margin, holding)
  arrays carried to the booster via `sample_weight`-adjacent slots.
- Negotiated-margin data ingestion for a meaningful per-SKU newsvendor α.
- Multi-round EM imputation with a stopping criterion based on test-set
  bias drift (rather than a fixed 1 round).
- Optuna-tuned per-channel specialists (one tuning per channel instead of
  reusing the global tune).
