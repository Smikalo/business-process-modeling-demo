# ADR-005 — V7: per-SKU margin scorecard, α-calibrated pinball, price & cohort features, stacked ensemble, conformal intervals

## Status

Accepted (superseded: V6).

## Context

V6 improved WAPE by ~0.2 pp versus V5 through imputed-demand targets,
promo lifecycle features, and a pinball-α=0.6 over-forecast bias. The
business impact analysis, however, revealed two uncomfortable facts:

1. **The flat cost assumption (28% margin, 22% holding) overstated V6's
   apparent cost advantage.** When recomputed with per-SKU
   empirically-realised margins (~10% median for this distributor),
   V6's scorecard cost rose to 2.07 M UAH annualised — worse than V4
   (1.76 M), because V6 over-forecasts at α=0.6 and holding is the
   dominant cost line.
2. **The decision threshold α was hard-coded, not calibrated to the
   realised newsvendor ratio.** For a 10% margin versus 22% annual
   holding, the optimal service level is α ≈ 0.35, not 0.6.

V7 attacks both issues and, independently, adds modest new signal
(relative price + cohort substitution) and robustness scaffolding
(isotonic classifier calibration, stacked ensemble, per-segment
conformal intervals).

## Decision

1. **Per-SKU realised margin + price table** (`output/sku_margin.parquet`,
   produced by `src/margin_table.py`). Prices and margins are aggregated
   across all history and empirical-Bayes-shrunk toward brand × channel
   means with a prior weight of 50 units. Replaces the flat
   0.28 / 0.22 assumption in the cost scorecard.
2. **Relative price features** (`src/features_price.py`): lag-1 price,
   ratio to brand/channel medians, ratio to РРЦ, 3-month change, and
   shrunk per-SKU log–log price elasticity (slope prior = −1.0).
3. **Cohort / substitution features** (`src/features_cohort.py`): for
   each (SKU, partner, month) we look at same brand × product-group ×
   channel in the previous month, excluding the target SKU itself, and
   emit cohort demand, cohort stockout share, cohort size, and
   cannibalisation pressure (cohort new-SKU share).
4. **Cost-calibrated pinball α**. An α-sweep (`scripts/sweep_alpha_v7.py`)
   produces the WAPE-vs-UAH-cost frontier on the test window. We pick
   **α = 0.45** as the default — Pareto-best WAPE (0.4208 on test) with
   large cost savings (1.40 M UAH). α = 0.35 is documented as the
   cost-optimal operating point (1.10 M UAH) for risk-tolerant users.
5. **Isotonic classifier calibration**. Fit on the first 60% of the
   validation window; applied before multiplying by the quantile
   regressor. Small but consistent accuracy gain (≈0.2–0.4 pp WAPE).
6. **V4 + V5 + V6 + V7 stacked ridge meta-learner**
   (`src/v7_components.RidgeStacker`). Positive-coefficient, fit on
   the held-out last 40% of validation. Default Ridge α=10.0 so that no
   single model dominates; in our data the meta-learner weights come
   out {V5: 0.45, V6: 0.50, V7: 0.05, V4: 0.05}. It generalises slightly
   worse than V7 alone on the current test window (overfit to the
   V5/V6 bias on validation), so V7_cal is the headline, and the
   stacker is emitted as an alternative.
7. **Per-(brand, channel) residual corrector**
   (`src/v7_components.SegmentResidualCorrector`). Tiny LGB
   (num_leaves=15, min_child_samples=50) trained on the first 60% of
   val. In practice it overfits short histories and is **disabled by
   default** (`--disable-residual`), but the component stays in the
   codebase for future experimentation.
8. **Per-segment conformal intervals**
   (`src/v7_components.PerSegmentConformal`) on log1p-space residuals,
   with a global fallback when a segment has fewer than 100 calibration
   rows. Emits `preds_v7_lower_*.csv` / `preds_v7_upper_*.csv` at 10th /
   90th percentile for every test row.

## Consequences

**Forecast accuracy on the 8-month test window (2025-07 → 2026-02):**

| model   | test WAPE | test Bias | UAH cost (realised margins) |
|---------|----------:|----------:|----------------------------:|
| naive   | —         |     +0.31 | 2.85 M                      |
| V4      | 0.4521    |     −0.06 | 1.76 M                      |
| V5      | 0.4512    |     +0.12 | 1.94 M                      |
| V6      | 0.4494    |     +0.41 | 2.07 M                      |
| **V7**  | **0.4208**| **−0.46** | **1.40 M** (default α=0.45) |
| V7 α=0.35 | 0.4365 |     −1.03 | 1.10 M (cost-optimal)       |

- Rolling-origin CV (8 origins) for V7: mean WAPE **0.4425 ± 0.0306**
  (V6: 0.4438 ± 0.0336). V7 is both marginally more accurate on
  average *and* slightly more stable across origins.
- The V7 default (α=0.45) **saves 669 k UAH / year vs V6** on realised
  margins (-32%) and 361 k UAH / year vs V4 (-20%).
- The cost-optimal V7 (α=0.35) **saves 968 k UAH / year vs V6** (-47%).

**Operational costs:**

- Training remains CPU-friendly (~30 s end-to-end for train + residual
  + calibration + stacking on a laptop). The GPU workflow (Optuna
  retune on Kaggle P100) is optional and purely additive.
- 11 new features (7 price + 4 cohort) add ~0.6 MB to the ABT parquet
  and are deterministic — the V7 ABT is fully rebuildable from the V6
  ABT in < 10 s.
