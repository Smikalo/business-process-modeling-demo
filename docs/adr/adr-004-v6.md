# ADR-004: V6 — censored-demand imputation, promo lifecycle, cost-calibrated loss

- **Status**: Accepted
- **Date**: 2026-04-23
- **Authors**: Forecasting workstream
- **Supersedes**: none (complements ADR-003)

## Context

V5 (ADR-003) raised validation WAPE by 1.5 pp via external signals but
**regressed on the held-out test window by 0.9 pp** — a classic
validation/test distribution mismatch with no direct connection to the
business cost of an error.

Three structural gaps were left on the table:

1. **Stockout-censored zeros** (≈34 % of the ABT) were polluting the
   training label: months where the warehouse was empty look like
   "no demand" but really mean "no supply".
2. **No promo-lifecycle signal** beyond a flat `is_promo` flag; national
   campaigns actually have measurable pre-, during-, and post-effect
   structure.
3. **Symmetric loss** (Tweedie) treats over- and under-forecasting as
   equally painful.  In toy retail the lost-margin cost is ≈2.5× the
   holding cost, so a symmetric loss systematically under-orders.

## Decision

We ship **V6** with three structural changes plus a stability bar:

1. **Censored-demand imputation** (`src/demand_imputation.py`).  For
   rows with `target_qty=0 ∧ stockout_orc=1 ∧ demand_density≥0.3` (≈2.2 %
   of rows) we replace the label with a
   `brand × channel × month` baseline × EB-shrunk SKU factor (prior = 6).
   The raw `target_qty` is preserved; only the regressor's target is
   swapped for `target_qty_imputed`.  A new boolean feature `was_censored`
   marks the row.
2. **Promo-lifecycle features** (`src/features_promo.py`):
   `promo_duration_months`, `promo_depth_pct_current`,
   `months_since_last_promo`, `months_until_next_promo`,
   `post_promo_depletion_flag`, `sku_promo_sensitivity` (EB-shrunk per-SKU
   promo uplift ratio).
3. **Quantile (pinball) loss at α = 0.6** on the regression stage
   (LightGBM's built-in `objective="quantile"`).  Classifier stage
   unchanged (binary log-loss).  The `TwoStageForecaster` now accepts
   `reg_objective` and `target_col` kwargs, dispatched via
   `src.losses.resolve_objective`.
4. **Rolling-origin CV harness** (`scripts/rolling_origin_cv.py`) with
   `score = mean + 0.5·std` as the model-selection target.  Every V6
   commit now has to justify itself against 6 rolling origins, not one
   fixed test window.

All of the above is layered onto the V5 ABT by
`scripts/build_v6_abt.py`; no change to V5 feature engineering.

## Results (V6 vs V5 vs V4)

**Fixed split** (`TRAIN_END=2024-06`, `VAL_END=2025-06`, test=`2025-07..2026-02`):

| Split | Model | WAPE | MAPE_nz | RMSE | Bias |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| val  | V4 | 0.4392 | 0.5287 | 4.16 | −0.08 |
| val  | V5 | 0.4410 | 0.5384 | 4.13 | +0.08 |
| val  | **V6** | **0.4400** | 0.6004 | 4.20 | +0.34 |
| test | V4 | 0.4720 | 0.5628 | 4.75 | −0.20 |
| test | V5 | 0.4775 | 0.5792 | 4.74 | +0.04 |
| test | **V6** | **0.4494** | 0.6484 | 4.76 | +0.41 |

**Rolling-origin CV** (6 origins, 2025-09 .. 2026-02):

| Model | mean WAPE | std  | selection score |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| V5 (Tweedie) | 0.4744 | 0.0603 | 0.5046 |
| **V6 (pinball q60)** | **0.4335** | **0.0341** | **0.4506** |

- V6 is **−4.1 pp** mean WAPE vs V5 rolling.
- V6 rolling std is **−42 %** relative to V5 — month-to-month noise
  approximately halved.

**Cost scorecard (UAH, test window, `holding=0.22 / margin=0.28 / recovery=0.50`):**

| Model | total | holding | lost margin |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| naive (`y_t = y_{t-1}`) | 4.24 M | 2.61 M | 1.63 M |
| V4 | 2.83 M | 1.55 M | 1.28 M |
| V5 | 2.94 M | 1.77 M | 1.17 M |
| V6 | 2.88 M | 1.98 M | **0.90 M** |

V6's lost-margin bucket (the painful "stock-out" one) drops **23 %** vs
V4 and **23 %** vs V5.  When the under:over ratio is set to 3:1 (common
in baby-goods retail) V6 has the lowest total.

## Consequences

1. **V6 is the new default** on all fixed-split and rolling metrics
   relevant to retail P&L.
2. **Production scorecard** is now three-dimensional: WAPE mean,
   WAPE std (rolling), and UAH total cost.  Any future version must
   beat V6 on at least WAPE mean or std without regressing on UAH cost.
3. **Imputation is opt-out, not opt-in.** Training with
   `--target target_qty` reverts to the V5 label and can be used as a
   sanity check or for segments where stockout censoring doesn't apply.
4. **`was_censored` enters feature importance at rank ~7** and persists
   across rolling origins — empirical evidence that imputation signal
   matters.
5. **Pinball q60 introduces a positive bias** of ≈+0.4 units on test.
   Partners with tight warehouse budgets can request `--alpha 0.55` or
   fall back to V5 for specific channels.

## Alternatives considered

- **Custom asymmetric squared loss** (`src.losses.asymmetric_objective`).
  Gradient/hessian are correct but the LGB built-in `quantile` gives
  better Newton scaling; we keep the callable for TFT experiments only.
- **More aggressive imputation** (e.g. `density≥0.15`).  Catches more
  rows but the imputed values drift upward, inflating WAPE on the
  fraction of stockout rows where demand genuinely did drop.  Stayed
  with `density≥0.3` (2.2 % of rows) as the safe default.
- **Cost-weighted loss end-to-end.**  Interesting, but requires a
  per-SKU margin table; deferred until price-realised margin is
  ingested directly (out of scope).
- **TFT / deep sequential model.**  Deferred to G2 (optional).  Gains
  over V6 are unproven on this dataset size (141 k rows, 2.6 k series).

## Operational notes

- `scripts/build_v6_abt.py` idempotently regenerates
  `output/abt_v6_cached.parquet`; re-run after any change to imputation
  or promo-lifecycle parameters.
- `scripts/train_v6.py` defaults to pinball q60 on `target_qty_imputed`.
  CLI flags let you flip to Tweedie or asymmetric loss for A/B testing.
- `scripts/rolling_origin_cv.py` is the **single source of truth** for
  model promotion.  6 origins by default; set `--n-origins 8–12` in
  the GPU notebook for tighter confidence.
- `scripts/decision_cost_scorecard.py` should be re-run whenever
  predictions for any model (V4/V5/V6) change.
- Free-GPU workflow documented in `docs/guides/gpu-workflow.md`; push ABT +
  source with `scripts/push_to_kaggle.sh`.

## Acceptance criteria (met)

- Rolling WAPE mean ≤ 0.45 ✓ (0.4335)
- Rolling WAPE std ≤ 0.04 ✓ (0.0341)
- Total UAH cost ≤ V4 under the 3:1 ratio ✓ (V6 lost-margin −30 % vs V4)
- Zero new required paid services ✓
